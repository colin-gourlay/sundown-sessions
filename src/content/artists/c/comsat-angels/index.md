## About

Comsat Angels (originally known as The Comsat Angels) are an English post-punk band from Sheffield, founded in 1978 and disbanded in 1995. The band are widely regarded as one of the leading acts to emerge from the post-punk scene, crafting a sound built around atmospheric guitar work, minimalist rhythms, and introspective lyrics.

The original lineup — Steve Fellows (vocals, guitar), Andy Peake (keyboards), Mik Glaisher (drums), and Kevin Bacon (bass) — signed to Polydor Records and released their debut album *Waiting for a Miracle* in 1980. Their subsequent albums *Sleep No More* (1981) and *Fiction* (1982) are considered landmarks of the post-punk genre. Bass duties later passed to Terry Todd, who appeared on several of the band's later recordings.

After shortening their name from "The Comsat Angels" to "Comsat Angels" in the late 1980s, the band continued to record and tour throughout the early 1990s. They released *My Mind's Eye* in 1992 before disbanding in 1995. The original lineup later reunited for a one-off performance at the Sensoria Festival in Sheffield in 2009.
