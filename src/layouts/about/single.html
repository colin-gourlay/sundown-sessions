An editorial introduction to Sundown Sessions

Some of the best music I have ever heard was not handed to me by an algorithm.

It came from recommendations, tiny venues, independent labels, late-night rabbit holes, old favourites, new obsessions, and records someone quietly insisted I should hear.

Sundown Sessions grew from that same belief: that discovering music should still feel personal, surprising and worth staying up for.

What Sundown Sessions is

An independent home for alternative music after dark.

Sundown Sessions is an independent home for alternative music after dark.

Each broadcast brings together new releases, overlooked artists, forgotten classics, deep cuts and songs that deserve a little more attention.

It is part radio show, part archive, part listening notebook - a place to hear the broadcasts, follow the track listings, explore featured artists and discover records that might otherwise pass you by.

Why after dark?

The right time to properly listen.

Sundown has always felt like the right time to properly listen.

The day slows down, distractions fade, and music has more room to breathe. That is the mood behind the show: curious, unhurried, atmospheric and open to the unexpected.

What you will discover

Music chosen by hand, not by algorithm.

You will find:

  • Carefully curated broadcasts
  • Complete track listings
  • Independent and emerging artists
  • New releases and overlooked records
  • Forgotten favourites and deep cuts
  • Artist and release pages for deeper exploration
  • Listen Again links for previous shows
  • Music chosen by hand, not by algorithm

How the music is chosen

Proper listening, followed by a thread.

Music comes from everywhere: labels, promoters, artists, listeners, Bandcamp, record shops, live shows, old favourites, new obsessions and the occasional happy accident.

Some tracks arrive with a press release. Some are found by following a thread from one artist to another. Some simply refuse to leave the room.

That is the heart of Sundown Sessions: human curation, proper listening and the belief that discovery should still feel exciting.
